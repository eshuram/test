Mahatma Gandhi

Leader of India’s non-violent freedom movement (1869–1948)

“Be the change you wish to see in the world.”

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, widely known as Mahatma (“great soul”), led India’s struggle for independence through non-violent resistance and civil disobedience. His ideas of ahimsa (non-violence) and satyagraha (truth-force) inspired civil rights movements around the world.

Biography

Early Life & Education

Gandhi was born into a Hindu Modh Bania family in Porbandar, Gujarat. In 1888, he traveled to London to study law at the Inner Temple. After qualifying as a barrister, he moved to South Africa in 1893, where he confronted racial discrimination and developed his philosophy of non-violent resistance.

Return to India & Independence Movement

Returning to India in 1915, Gandhi emerged as a leader of the Indian National Congress. He organized mass movements such as the Non-Cooperation Movement (1920–22), the Salt March to Dandi (1930) protesting the salt tax, and the Quit India Movement (1942) demanding an end to British rule.

Philosophy: Ahimsa & Satyagraha

Gandhi’s methods emphasized moral courage and the power of collective, disciplined non-violence. He believed that just means are essential to just ends, advocating simple living, self-reliance, and communal harmony.

Legacy

Gandhi’s life and ideas transformed politics and ethics, inspiring global civil rights movements. India observes Gandhi Jayanti on October 2, a day also recognized as the International Day of Non-Violence.
